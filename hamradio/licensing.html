Licensing Information

There are three different tiers of ham radio licenses. The first tier is the Technician license, second is the General license, and the final is the Amateur Extra license. Each level of license requires passing a written multiple-choice test, and the levels must be taken in order. By completing the requirements for a higher level license there are expanded options for frequency ranges that can be used for communication.

Technician License

The Technician license is the lowest tier license with the easiest test. The written test includes questions about electronics and radio theory, operation of a ham radio, and federal regulations. It grants operating privileges in most of the upper radio frequencies of the VHF and UHF bands, and limited privileges in a few of the lower bands.

General License

The General license is the middle tier license. Its written test includes questions regarding more advanced concepts, regulations, and practices. Holding this tier of license allows much more open radio usage, especially on many of the HF bands used for long distance communication around the world.

Amateur Extra License

The Amateur Extra license is the highest tier license available. People who hold this license are expected to be experts in the world of ham radio, so its written test is all about the most advanced concepts and regulations. Achieving this level of license allows wide-open access to all of the frequency bands available.
